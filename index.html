Door Supervisor - Qualification Level 2

The SIA Door Supervisor Level 2 Qualification is one of our most demanding qualifications for individuals seeking to work in the private security industry. This qualification allows individuals to work in roles involving licensed premises where alcohol is served publicly. A Door Supervisor can perform all the duties of a Security Guard, whereas a Security Guard cannot work as a Door Supervisor. The training is highly beneficial as it not only opens doors to employment in the thriving security sector but also builds confidence, communication, and problem-solving skills.

Who is this qualification for?

This qualification is aimed at individuals who are looking to become qualified to obtain a full frontline Door Supervisor SIA licence. By undertaking this qualification, you will meet the requirements to be deployed within Security roles undertaking licencable activities under the terms of the Private Security Act (2001)

Course Content

To achieve the qualification learners must achieve 6 credits out of 4 Units

Unit 1: Principles of Working in the Private Security Industry

110 Minute Exam – 72 Questions (70% Pass Mark) | Completion of written Evidential Statement

  • Private Security Industry: The Legal Aspects
  • Health and Safety for Private Security Operatives
  • Fire Safety At Work Awareness
  • Emergency Procedures
  • Communication Skills and Customer Care

Unit 2: Principles of Working as a Door Supervisor in the Private Security Industry

75 Minute Exam – 50 Questions (70% Pass Mark) | Practical Scenario Assessments & Use of Communications Devices

  • Behavioural Standards
  • Civil and Criminal Law
  • Searching
  • Understand Arrest Procedures
  • Drugs Awareness
  • Recording Incidents and Crime Scene Preservation
  • Licensing Law
  • Emergency Procedures

Unit 3: Application of Conflict Management skills in the Private Security Industry

30 Minute Exam – 20 Questions (70% Pass Mark) | Conflict Management Scenario Assessments

  • Avoiding Conflict and Reducing Personal Risk
  • Defusing Conflicts
  • Resolving and Learning from Conflicts
  • Application of Communication Skills and Conflict Management for Door Supervisors

Unit 4: Application of Physical Intervention skills in the Private Security Industry

45 Minute Exams – 30 Questions (80% Pass Mark) | Physical Intervention Scenario Assessments

  • Introduction to Physical Intervention Skills
  • Need-to-Know Legislation

Eligibility

To be eligible to undertake the training course, candidates must be able to evidence the following.

  • Candidates must be 18 years or older
  • Must be able to meet the ID requirements set by the SIA
  • Must hold a valid Emergency First Aid certificate that meets the requirements of the Health & Safety (First Aid) Regulations 1981*
  • Undertake online basic English assessments achieving appropriate results

*If you require EFAW training, we can provide this for an additional charge when booking onto our Door Supervisor training course.

For further guidance on undertaking our licence-linked qualifications and meeting the requirements for obtaining an SIA licence such as ID requirements, DBS checks etc. please use the Security Industry Authority. Funding may be available for this course. Please get in touch and a member of our team will be happy to assist.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does an SIA licence cost and can VIRPIO SECURITY assist with funding?

£184 for your initial frontline licence. In some cases, we are able to assist you with the funding of your licence depending on your residency status and earnings or your current economic situation. Speak to our representative about the options available to you.

I have criminal convictions; can I still apply for an SIA licence?

This is not at the discretion of VIRPIO SECURITY. The SIA website has a criminal records indicator which will give you some idea as to whether or not you would be eligible to apply for a licence.

How long does my Level 2 Door Supervisor Qualification last for?

Your Level 2 Door Supervisor qualification typically lasts for 3 years from the date you successfully complete the training and assessment and attained the physical licence. After 3 years, you'll need to renew your qualification in order to continue working as a door supervisor legally. This process involves completing a Refresher Course and re-taking any necessary assessments. It is also important to ensure that your SIA licence details are up to date.

How long does it take to get my SIA licence?

The Security Industry Authority aims to process all applications within 25 working days. However, on average the entire process often takes around 6 weeks.

What if I fail elements of my training?

VIRPIO SECURITY will provide candidates with one free re-sit (funded candidates only) for formal examinations. This will be subject to a threshold being met through mock exams and at the discretion of your course tutor. Any further re-sits will be at a cost of £50 per module.

Do I have to renew my licence?

Your licence will expire three years from the date it was issued. Once it expires, you cannot legally work as a door supervisor. If you wish to continue working, you must renew it. You can begin renewing your current SIA Door Supervisor Licence up to four months before its expiration. The new licence will only become active once your existing licence has expired. Please note that the application fee is £184 and is non-refundable.

Can I work with VIRPIO SECURITY?

Upon successfully completing the Door Supervisor SIA training with VIRPIO SECURITY, learners are guaranteed an interview opportunity. If the candidate demonstrates suitability in line with the job description, the job will be offered. VIRPIO SECURITY is committed to supporting its learners in securing employment, ensuring they have the best possible chance to transition from training to a professional role in the security industry. This commitment reflects the company's dedication to the success and career progression of its trainees.
